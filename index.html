About Me

A highly self-motivated, detail-oriented, and forward-thinking Electrical Engineer with a strong passion for advancing next-generation automotive and robotic technologies. I am deeply driven by innovation in autonomous vehicles, ADAS, software-defined vehicles (SDVs), electric vehicles (EVs), and intelligent robotics. Through rigorous research and hands-on, solution-driven projects, I have developed expertise in V2X communication, Battery Management Systems (BMS), Electronic Control Units (ECUs), PCB design, and AI-powered perception and control systems. I bring a creative mindset, analytical problem-solving skills, and a relentless curiosity to every challenge in the evolving automotive and robotics landscape.

Research

  • Hybrid V2X Communication: Combining Visible Light Communication (VLC) and C-V2X to enable robust vehicle-to-vehicle and vehicle-to-infrastructure communication.
    • Designed a dual-channel architecture using VLC for short-range, high-precision line-of-sight communication and C-V2X for non-line-of-sight, long-range communication.
    • Investigated cybersecurity vulnerabilities in hybrid systems including spoofing and signal jamming, focusing on authentication and secure key exchange mechanisms.
    • Simulated communication under adverse weather and lighting conditions to evaluate hybrid reliability and latency.
